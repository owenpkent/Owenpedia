# Transparency of Care

## Purpose

This document ensures open communication and transparency between Owen, family members, and caregivers regarding all aspects of care, medical decisions, and daily routines.

## Core Principles

### Open Communication
- All caregivers should feel comfortable asking questions
- Owen's preferences and comfort are the priority
- No question is too small or insignificant
- Regular check-ins to ensure understanding
- Owen will talk you through his routine

### Informed Decision Making
- Owen is involved in all care decisions
- Medical information is shared clearly and completely
- Caregivers understand the "why" behind procedures
- Alternative approaches are discussed when appropriate

### Accountability
- Clear documentation of care activities
- Honest reporting of any issues or concerns
- Timely communication of changes or problems
- Shared responsibility for Owen's wellbeing

## Communication Guidelines

### Daily Updates
- Report any changes in condition or routine
- Document all care activities accurately
- Share observations about comfort, pain, or concerns
- Communicate equipment issues immediately

### Questions & Concerns
- Ask for clarification when unsure about procedures
- Report any discomfort or problems immediately
- Suggest improvements to care routines
- Discuss scheduling or logistical challenges

### Information Sharing
- All caregivers have access to care documentation
- Medical updates are shared promptly
- Changes to routines are communicated in advance
- Emergency procedures are clearly understood

## Caregiver Expectations

### What Caregivers Should Know
- Complete understanding of daily routines (allow 2 hours for morning routine)
- Proper use of all medical equipment (lift, sling, feeding pump, ventilator)
- Emergency procedures and contacts
- Owen's preferences and communication style
- Owen will guide you through his care preferences

### What Caregivers Should Do
- Follow established care protocols
- Document care activities accurately
- Communicate proactively about concerns
- Maintain professional boundaries while being personable
- Complete all shift and household responsibilities before leaving

### What Caregivers Should Report
- Any changes in Owen's condition
- Equipment malfunctions or concerns
- Supply needs or shortages
- Scheduling conflicts or availability changes

## Medical Transparency

### Treatment Decisions
- Owen is informed of all medical recommendations
- Risks and benefits are clearly explained
- Alternative options are discussed
- Owen's preferences guide decision-making

### Medication Management
- Clear understanding of all medications and purposes
- Side effects are monitored and reported
- Changes are communicated and documented
- Questions about medications are encouraged

### Equipment & Procedures
- Proper training on all equipment
- Understanding of safety protocols
- Regular maintenance and inspection
- Immediate reporting of issues

## Privacy & Respect

### Maintaining Dignity
- Owen's privacy is respected at all times
- Personal information is kept confidential
- Care is provided with dignity and respect
- Owen's autonomy is honored
- If comfortable, assist with personal wellness routines

### Professional Boundaries
- Appropriate caregiver-client relationship
- Respectful communication
- Cultural sensitivity
- Personal space and preferences honored

## Problem Resolution

### Addressing Concerns
1. Identify the issue clearly
2. Communicate directly with relevant parties
3. Seek solutions collaboratively
4. Document resolution and follow-up

### Escalation Process
- Minor issues: Discuss with Owen or primary caregiver
- Equipment problems: Contact supplier immediately
- Medical concerns: Contact healthcare provider
- Emergencies: Call 911

## Documentation Standards

### Required Documentation
- Daily care logs
- Medication administration records
- Feeding schedules and amounts
- Equipment maintenance logs
- Incident reports when necessary

### Accuracy & Honesty
- Record information truthfully
- Document in a timely manner
- Include relevant details
- Sign and date all entries

## Continuous Improvement

### Feedback Welcome
- Caregivers can suggest routine improvements
- Owen provides feedback on care quality
- Regular review of procedures and protocols
- Adaptation to changing needs

### Training & Education
- Ongoing caregiver education
- Updates on new equipment or procedures
- Sharing of best practices
- Professional development opportunities

## Night Care Expectations

### Sleep Monitoring
- Baby monitor must be on and volume up while sleeping
- Respond when Owen calls out during the night
- Owen may wake 1-5 times needing assistance
- Common needs: covers adjusted, suction, turning over, leg/arm adjustments, pillow adjustments

### Being Present
- Stay within earshot during toileting
- Respond promptly to calls
- Maintain awareness throughout shift
- Ensure Owen's comfort and safety

## Shift Completion Standards

Before leaving each shift, ensure:
- All shift responsibilities completed
- All household responsibilities completed
- Kitchen clean and tidy
- Beds made, rooms tidy
- Documentation up to date
- Any concerns communicated
