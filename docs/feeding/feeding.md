# Feeding Guide

Owen uses a feeding tube (G-tube) with a Mickey button for nutrition.

---

## Equipment

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Mickey Button | Port in Owen's stomach for tube feeding |
| Feeding Tube | Extension that connects to Mickey button |
| Feeding Bag | Holds the formula |
| Feeding Machine | Pumps formula at controlled rate |
| Syringes | For medications and manual feeding |
| Beakers | For measuring water |

---

## Feeding Formula

### Standard Mix

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Liquid Hope | 1 pouch |
| Water | 1 pouch |
| Kate Farms | 2 boxes |
| Water | 2 boxes |

**Fill the feeding bag completely to the very top.** Make sure cap is fastened well to prevent spills.

### Flow Rate
150-180 milliliters per hour

---

## Morning Feeding Setup

1. Attach feeding tube to Owen's stomach Mickey button
2. Give **6.6 mL Evrysdi** (use orange syringe) followed by tall beaker of water
3. Prepare feeding formula (see mix above)
4. Attach feeding bag to carabiner on wheelchair
5. Place in black bag, attach to feeding machine
6. Turn on machine (hold button until numbers stop changing)
7. Prime until food reaches tip
8. Attach to feeding tube
9. Press **"Run"**

---

## Lunch Refill (~Noon)

- Add 2 Kate Farms
- Add 2 waters

---

## Evening/Bedtime

1. Detach feeding tube from feeding bag tube
2. Remove feeding tube from Mickey button
3. Clean feeding tube with warm water syringes (no soap)
4. Leave on paper towel to dry overnight in kitchen
5. Discard feeding bag (unless Owen says otherwise)

---

## Syringe Feeding

For morning wake-up:
- 1 syringe Kate Farms box food
- 1 syringe water

---

## Cleaning

- Clean all syringes and beakers with **warm water only** (no soap)
- Clean stomach Mickey button area with split sponge during shower
