# Curried Stuffed Peppers

Bell peppers stuffed with curried turkey or sausage and grains.

**Total Time:** 45 minutes  
**Servings:** 4

---

## Ingredients

- 4 large bell peppers (any color), tops removed, cored and seeded
- 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
- ½ lb ground turkey or mild/spicy turkey sausage (casings removed)
- 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- 1 zucchini, finely chopped
- ½ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
- ¼ cup dried currants
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- ½ cup plain rice milk, soy milk, or almond milk
- 1 tbsp mild curry powder
- ¼ tsp fine sea salt
- ¼ tsp ground black pepper
- 4 cups cooked millet or quinoa, hot
- 1 lemon, cut into wedges (optional)

---

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 350°F
2. Bring large pot of water to boil
3. Add peppers and cook for 3 minutes
4. Drain peppers and run under cold water until completely cool; set aside
5. Heat oil in large skillet over medium heat
6. Add onions and cook until soft, about 3 minutes
7. Add turkey or sausage and garlic
8. Cook, breaking up turkey with wooden spoon, until evenly browned, about 5 minutes
9. Add zucchini, cilantro, currants, and lemon juice
10. Cover and simmer over low heat for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally
11. Remove skillet from heat and set aside
12. Arrange peppers in shallow baking dish, hollow-side up, touching each other
13. Using slotted spoon, divide turkey mixture between peppers (reserve liquid in skillet)
14. Bring reserved liquid in skillet to boil over medium-high heat
15. Reduce heat to low
16. Whisk in rice/soy/almond milk, curry powder, salt, and pepper
17. Simmer, whisking constantly, until curry powder dissolves, 1-2 minutes
18. Spoon sauce evenly over each stuffed pepper
19. Cover dish with aluminum foil
20. Bake for 25-30 minutes until peppers are tender
21. To serve, divide millet or quinoa among 4 plates
22. Arrange peppers on top
23. Spoon any liquid from baking dish over peppers
24. Garnish with lemon wedges if desired

---

## Notes

- Can be made vegetarian by substituting lentils or more grains for turkey
- Leftovers reheat well
