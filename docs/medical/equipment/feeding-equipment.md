# Feeding Equipment

## Overview

Owen's feeding equipment delivers nutrition through a G-tube feeding system. For detailed feeding procedures, see the [Feeding Guide](../../feeding/feeding.md).

---

## Equipment Components

### Feeding Machine (Pump)
- **Purpose:** Pumps formula at controlled rate
- **Rate:** 150-180 milliliters per hour
- **Power:** Rechargeable battery
- **Charging:** Plug in at night

### Feeding Bag
- **Purpose:** Holds formula
- **Capacity:** Fill completely full to the very top
- **Attachment:** Attaches to carabiner on wheelchair
- **Storage:** Keep in black bag on wheelchair
- **Disposal:** Discard nightly (unless Owen says otherwise)

### Mickey Button
- **Type:** Stomach port (G-tube)
- **Purpose:** Access point for feeding tube
- **Care:** Clean area well during shower with split sponge

### Feeding Tube
- **Type:** Extension tube for Mickey button
- **Purpose:** Connects feeding bag to Mickey button
- **Care:** Clean nightly with warm water (no soap)
- **Storage:** Leave on paper towel to dry overnight in kitchen

---

## Daily Usage

### Morning
1. Reattach feeding tube to Owen's stomach Mickey button
2. Give Evrysdi medication through feeding tube
3. Prepare feeding formula
4. Fill feeding bag completely full
5. Attach to feeding machine and wheelchair
6. Prime line and start feeding

### Throughout Day
- Monitor feeding rate
- Check for any issues or alarms
- Refill at lunch (2 Kate Farms + 2 waters)

### Evening
1. Detach feeding tube from feeding bag
2. Remove feeding tube from Mickey button
3. Clean feeding tube with warm water (no soap)
4. Leave to dry overnight on paper towel in kitchen
5. Discard feeding bag (unless Owen says otherwise)
6. Plug in feeding pump to charge

---

## Formula Preparation

### Standard Formula Mix
- 1 pouch Liquid Hope
- 1 pouch water
- 2 boxes Kate Farms
- 2 boxes water

**Important:** Fill feeding bag completely full right to the very top of bag. Make sure cap is fastened well or it can easily spill.

---

## Maintenance

### Daily Cleaning
- Clean feeding tube with warm water (no soap)
- Clean all syringes and beakers with warm water (no soap)
- Leave feeding tube to dry overnight

### Equipment Care
- Charge feeding pump nightly
- Check for any leaks or damage
- Ensure all connections are secure
- Replace feeding bags as directed

---

## Safety

### Important Notes
- Always pinch feeding tube closed when attaching/detaching
- Ensure cap is fastened securely to prevent spills
- Check that feeding machine is running properly
- Monitor for any alarms or issues
