# Pozole

Traditional Mexican hominy stew with pork and red chile sauce.

**Servings:** 8-10

---

## Ingredients

### For the Soup
- 4 oz dried guajillo, ancho, or combination of both chile pods
- Salt
- 1 large (108 oz, 6 lb 12 oz) can white hominy, drained and rinsed
- 3 lbs pork shoulder (preferably with bone), cut into 1 to 1½ inch cubes
- 8 cloves garlic (4 roughly chopped, 4 whole)
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 2 tbsp dry oregano (Mexican oregano if available)

### Garnishes
- Half a small cabbage, thinly sliced
- 1 bunch cilantro, chopped
- ½ white onion, chopped
- 2 avocados, chopped
- 4 limes, quartered
- A bunch of red radishes, sliced thin
- A couple dozen tostada shells

---

## Instructions

### Prepare the Base
1. Fill a large 10-12 quart stockpot with 5 quarts water
2. Set on heat to bring to a boil

### Prepare the Chiles
1. Remove and discard stems, seeds, and large veins from chile pods
2. Heat cast iron pan on medium-high
3. Lightly roast chile pods for a couple minutes until they soften (don't burn)
4. Bring 3 cups water to a boil in medium pot
5. Remove from heat, add chiles, cover
6. Let soak 15-20 minutes

### Brown the Pork
1. Heat 1-2 tbsp olive oil in large sauté pan over medium-high heat
2. Pat pork pieces dry with paper towels
3. Sprinkle generously with salt
4. Working in batches, brown meat on all sides (don't crowd the pan)
5. Near the end, add 4 cloves roughly chopped garlic
6. Let cook with meat for about 1 minute

### Build the Soup
1. Transfer browned pork to stockpot of boiling water
2. Scrape up browned bits and garlic from pan, add to pot
3. Add rinsed hominy
4. Add bay leaves, cumin, and oregano (crush oregano in your hands as you add it)
5. Add 1 tbsp salt
6. Bring to simmer, reduce heat, cook for 15 minutes

### Make the Red Sauce
1. In blender, purée chiles, 2½ cups soaking liquid, 1 tsp salt, and 4 whole garlic cloves
2. Start with chiles, garlic, and 1 cup liquid, then add rest of liquid
3. Strain sauce through sieve, discard tough bits

### Finish the Pozole
1. Add red chile sauce to pot with pork and hominy
2. Add another 2 tsp salt
3. Return to simmer
4. Lower heat to maintain simmer, partially cover
5. Cook 2-3 hours until pork is completely tender
6. Skim away excess fat
7. Taste and add more salt as needed (may need 1 tbsp or more)
8. Add more water if needed (should be brothy for garnishes)

### Serve
1. Prep garnishes: slice cabbage, chop cilantro, etc.
2. Arrange garnishes in bowls on table
3. Serve pozole in bowls
4. Let guests add their preferred garnishes
5. Serve with tostada shells or tortilla chips

---

## Notes

- This is a time-intensive recipe but worth it
- Can be made a day ahead - flavor improves
- Freezes well
